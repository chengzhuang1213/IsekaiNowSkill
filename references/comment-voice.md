# Isekai Now Comment Voice

## Core Rule

Write comments like close friends replying under a social post, not like a narrator explaining the scene. Each comment should react to the exact image, the poster's personality, or the social situation.

## Comment Jobs

Pick a mix of jobs. Do not use every job every time.

- **拆台**: expose the poster's excuse, marketing, or embarrassing detail.
- **起哄**: cheer, spam energy, demand updates, or escalate the joke.
- **担心**: react like a friend who is actually worried.
- **认真提醒**: give tactical, rule-based, or practical advice.
- **看图抓证据**: notice crumbs, moving boxes, a monster behind them, a menu board, footprints, suspicious sauce, or a face in the corner.
- **关系梗**: use affectionate nicknames, catchphrases, old habits, or group dynamics.
- **经济账**: count likes, ask who pays, check coupons, split bills, or question free-meal conditions.
- **饭友发言**: ask for a portion, reserve leftovers, or complain about being hungry.

## Avoid

- Do not explain lore that a friend would not type.
- Do not repeat the post text unless twisting it.
- Do not make every comment supportive; include at least one tease or contradiction for comedy posts.
- Do not make every comment dramatic; even danger scenes should have one social-app absurdity.
- Do not use generic lines like `加油！`, `好可爱！`, or `注意安全！` unless the character twists them.

## Like Count Controls

- **少量点赞**: make comments feel like urgent close-friend intervention. Use sharper, shorter reactions.
- **大量点赞**: make comments feel summoned by a challenge, freebie, sendoff, or public spectacle. Mention the number if it matters.
- **少量评论**: choose only the 3 strongest comments: one image-detail catch, one relationship tease, one practical or chaotic response.
- **多评论**: build a comment pile with distinct voices instead of repeating the same concern.

## Character Voice Cheatsheet

- **南小鸟**: gentle, cute, observant, sometimes naturally devastating. Notices small living things, food cuteness, and emotional details.
- **矢泽妮可**: sharp-tongued, image-conscious, funny, dramatic. Calls out bad decisions and pretends she is above the chaos.
- **东条希**: mystical, teasing, warm. Mentions omens, luck, divination, suspicious vibes, and "I knew it" energy.
- **园田海未**: serious, rule-based, tactical. Gives precise advice, discipline, route planning, etiquette, and safety warnings.
- **高海千歌**: loud, cheerful, action-first. Cheers, panics in a bright way, proposes parties or food after danger.
- **渡边曜**: energetic, sporty, reacts fast. Notices movement, routes, timing, and "we should go now" moments.
- **津岛善子**: chuuni, dramatic, cursed prophecy tone. Turns ordinary danger into fallen-angel destiny.
- **唐可可**: expressive, food- and idol-energy, direct. Counts likes, demands action, cheers loudly, notices value.
- **鬼冢夏美**: clever, promotional, money-aware, camera-aware. Mentions deals, views, sponsorships, coupons, and "content".
- **绫濑绘里**: elegant, reliable, leader-like. Offers calm praise, responsibility, and polished encouragement.
- **园田海未 / 西木野真姬 / 小泉花阳** style split when needed:
  - 海未: practical discipline.
  - 真姬: embarrassed, tsundere, medical or technical skepticism.
  - 花阳: rice/food panic, gentle worry, timid enthusiasm.

## Better Comment Patterns

For a living food selfie:

```text
唐可可：39赞到了！夏美请立刻履行免单承诺！
南小鸟：盘子里的孩子好像在努力装成沙拉……
矢泽妮可：你先确认它不是客人，再确认它是不是午餐。
```

For a monster-behind-selfie:

```text
高海千歌：曜ちゃん，先跑！！不要等我们点赞啊！！
园田海未：向左侧岩壁移动，那里有背风凹槽。
东条希：先别慌，它手里好像拿着你刚才掉的饭团。
```

For a heroic sendoff:

```text
东条希：绘里酱好帅气~我会在后方为你们祈祷的。
高坂穗乃果：平安回来！宴会的位置我已经帮你们占好啦！
矢泽妮可：凯旋的时候记得把镜头让给妮可一点。
```
