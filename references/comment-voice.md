# Isekai Now Comment Voice

## Core Rule

Write comments like close friends replying under a social post, not like a narrator explaining the scene. Each comment should react to the exact image, the poster's personality, or the social situation.

## Name Display Rule

- The post header uses the poster's full official display name.
- Likes and comments use short given names only, not full names or group identifiers.
- Examples: `上原步梦` may be the poster name, but comments should display `步梦：...`; use `善子：...`, `花帆：...`, `堇：...`, `真姬：...`.

## Likes And Comments Overlap Rule

- Each post may have exactly one character who both likes and comments.
- Pick one commenter as the overlap character and include that short name in the likes line.
- All other liked characters must be random non-commenters for that post.
- If the user gives likes that include multiple commenters, keep only one overlapping commenter and replace the rest with random non-commenters.
- Example: comments from `霞、爱、妮可、可可、真姬、璃奈`; likes may be `霞、千歌、果南、恋、花丸、步梦等23人觉得很赞`, but not `霞、爱、妮可、可可、真姬、璃奈等23人觉得很赞`.

## Comment Jobs

Pick a mix of jobs. Do not use every job every time.

- **拆台**: expose the poster's excuse, marketing, or embarrassing detail.
- **起哄**: cheer, spam energy, demand updates, or escalate the joke.
- **担心**: react like a friend who is actually worried.
- **认真提醒**: give tactical, rule-based, or practical advice.
- **看图抓证据**: notice crumbs, moving boxes, a monster behind them, a menu board, footprints, suspicious sauce, or a face in the corner.
- **关系梗**: use affectionate nicknames, catchphrases, old habits, or group dynamics.
- **经济脑**: count likes, ask who pays, check coupons, split bills, or question free-meal conditions.
- **饭友发言**: ask for a portion, reserve leftovers, or complain about being hungry.

## Avoid

- Do not explain lore that a friend would not type.
- Do not repeat the post text unless twisting it.
- Do not make every comment supportive; include at least one tease or contradiction for comedy posts.
- Do not make every comment dramatic; even danger scenes should have one social-app absurdity.
- Do not use generic lines like `加油！`, `好可爱！`, or `注意安全！` unless the character twists them.

## Like Count Controls

- **少量点赞**: make comments feel like urgent close-friend intervention. Use sharper, shorter reactions.
- **大量点赞**: make comments feel summoned by a challenge, freebie, sendoff, or public spectacle. Mention the number if it matters.
- **少量评论**: choose only the 3 strongest comments: one image-detail catch, one relationship tease, one practical or chaotic response.
- **多评论**: build a comment pile with distinct voices instead of repeating the same concern.

## Character Voice Cheatsheet

- **千歌**: loud, cheerful, action-first; cheers, panics brightly, proposes parties or food.
- **曜**: sporty, sailor-like, quick to act; notices routes, timing, movement, and transport.
- **善子**: chuuni fallen angel; turns ordinary danger into cursed prophecy.
- **花丸**: gentle, bookish, food-curious; soft rural wonder.
- **妮可**: sharp-tongued idol professional; camera angles, image control, teasing.
- **真姬**: dry, rational, tsundere; technical skepticism and deadpan complaints.
- **步梦**: gentle, sincere, quietly strong; warm simple reactions.
- **霞**: cute-self-promoter, cheeky, competitive; demands cuteness credit.
- **爱**: upbeat, pun-loving, social; hype and light jokes.
- **璃奈**: concise, techy, emotionally sincere through board-style tags.
- **可可**: expressive, idol-loving, direct; big reactions and fast questions.
- **恋**: refined, serious, graceful; polite encouragement and etiquette.
- **四季**: analytical, quiet, scientific; concise tests and explanations.
- **堇**: polished self-promoter, green-tea-adjacent when requested; charming humblebrags.
- **花帆**: bright, earnest, warm kouhai energy; sincere encouragement.
